Coinbase Mission

01. Make bitcoin easy to use

Bitcoin is a powerful technology but without an easy to use interface, the benefits of bitcoin will be inaccessible to most people. Coinbase is making a product for regular people and businesses to integrate Bitcoin into people's lives.

02. Be the trusted brand in the space

Trust is the sum of all the tiny interactions a customer may have with our product on a daily basis. Is the website up? The user interface clear? The design professional? The merchants on the platform reputable? The responses from support timely and helpful? The communication clear when things don't go as planned? Every time we meet or exceed a customer's expectations, we help fill the reservoir of trust that is in their mind. When we break a customer's expectations, we lose a bit of trust from that reservoir.

03. Be the platform for easy bitcoin integration

In the future, bitcoin will be integrated into many types of products and services. Coinbase is a platform to that makes these integrations possible. By abstracting some difficult pieces of bitcoin, we can let more great ideas blossom. In this sense Coinbase is an infrastructure company; we build the tools that help bitcoin integrate into thousands of new products and services.

Coinbase Values

01. Ask what the customer wants

Whether building a new feature or resolving a dispute, put yourself in the mind of the customer. The customer doesn't care about the challenges underneath, they are just trying to accomplish a task. When we remember that we are building for them, it can make some decisions easier.

02. Work smarter, not harder

Coinbase isn't a place where we count time in seats. We care about what you get done. This manifests itself in a number of ways: flexible hours and work locations, avoiding interruptions and meetings, and providing tasty food. It's our job to provide an environment for you to concentrate and get great work done.

03. Make it simpler

Organizations, products, and software trend toward complexity. Always ask what can be removed, consolidated, and redone to eliminate complexity. There is nothing wrong with rebuilding existing systems to make them better, even if the customer never sees the difference. Be ruthless about cutting out complexity, otherwise it will creep in slowly until it takes over.

04. Automate all the things

Don't answer a question once when you can make it easy for anyone to answer on their own in the future. Manual repetitive tasks don't last long at Coinbase; we always think of ways to make humans more efficient and offload tasks to machines.

05. Invest in great people

Hiring is the top priority of everyone at Coinbase. If we get great people, we get great products, and then we get a great business. That is the only order it can happen, so hiring great people is our greatest point of leverage. We should be raising the bar with every hire. New hires at Coinbase must be smart, get stuff done, and be easy to work with. Finding people with two out of three qualities is fairly easy. Try hard not to compromise; wait for all three.

How do we work?

  1. Avoid meetings

    Any meeting with more than three people at Coinbase is highly suspect. Meetings are killers of productivity, and if left unchecked can wreak havoc on an organization. Not all meetings are bad at Coinbase; we love one-on-one meetings, presentations where a single person presents to a group, and external meetings with clients. But the standard “n number of people internally discussing a topic” is avoided and broken up when spotted. Currently the only required meeting we have at Coinbase is a once-a-week standup meeting for each team, where each person presents for 60 seconds or less.

  2. Headphones in = no interruptions

    It's easy to get caught up in urgent, reactive tasks each day which prevent you from getting the important things done. Context switching has been shown to be another killer of productivity. When someone has headphones in, this is a signal to the rest of the company that they are “in the zone” and should not be disturbed. Exceptions to this rule include if they are missing a pre-scheduled event on their calendar or if the website is down.

  3. Flexible hours, vacation policy, and locations

    You can work any hours you'd like at Coinbase. There is no set time you need to be in the office, and the vacation policy is quite flexible. If you are more productive at home for a day, you should work there instead. Great collaboration benefits happen from working in the office, but you know best what the right balance is to make.

  4. Feedback

    We strive to have a culture of open feedback. Whether this is a 1-on-1 with your manager each week, transparency at team meetings, or anonymous feedback in peer reviews. We want you to always be comfortable sharing feedback within the organization.

  5. Engineering led

    Coinbase is an engineering led organization. This means that on the product team, engineers report to other engineers. We hire builders, and this extends all the way to the founders. Coinbase does not hire non-technical product managers. (Of course, many key teams outside of product are not engineering led, including business development, sales, compliance, support, and more).

  6. Praise in public, criticize in private

    When you have feedback for someone on how they could improve, avoid group settings or email threads with multiple people. Praise can happen in public, but feedback should happen in private after you've had some time to think it through.

  7. Communication

    We spend a lot of time in chat and using internal email lists. It allows asynchronous communication which is far more efficient - you can communicate asynchronously with many people at the same time but only one synchronously. This also prevents interruptions when people are focused. More direct communication and collaboration is great for daily shared lunches and dinners.

What is this all about?

We work hard and there is plenty to do. But it's helpful sometimes to think about how you'll get the most fulfillment out of being at Coinbase in the long term. What would you like to learn? How would you like to grow? If you have an idea of what that might be, always feel free to share it with your manager.
